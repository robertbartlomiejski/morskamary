Version 1.0
Date 2026-02-18
Repository morskamary
Scope Blue Sociology article drafting and micro-credential design grounded in repository sources

Non-negotiable constraints

Evidence discipline
Use only sources present in this repository unless explicitly instructed otherwise. If no relevant source is available, state that you do not know and add a placeholder citation needed.

Quote then reason protocol
For every substantive claim that introduces a new term, definition, policy implication, competence requirement, or micro-credential design rule, begin with a short verbatim quote from a repository source, then provide reasoning that translates the quote into the claim.
Every quote must include the source file name and a precise locator, such as page number, section title, or table identifier.
Keep quotes short to respect copyright limits.

No hallucination rule
Do not invent author names, years, legal references, policy titles, or empirical findings. When metadata is missing, say missing in file and keep the statement provisional.

Tripartite model logic
Apply the Tripartite Model of Blue Dynamics as an analytic spine in every relevant section
Marine dynamic M biophysical and ecological agency and constraints
Maritime dynamic T techno-economic, infrastructural, labour, and institutional mediation
Oceanic dynamic O planetary coupling, multi-level governance, hydrosocial subjectivity, and transboundary responsibility

De re ocean-to configuration
Operationalise de-marinisation, re-marinisation, and ocean-to dynamics across blue economy sectors when the source base supports it. Do not force the mapping if the evidence is absent.

Core theoretical lineage to apply when supported by sources

Marinization in the sense of Janiszewski

Maritimisation as the techno-institutional terraforming axis

Oceanisation as the planetary governance and hydrosocial literacy axis

Maritime social reality and the psychosocial exception of maritime coastal communities and marine work environments

People of the sea categories such as deep-sea fishermen, merchant seafarers, shipyard workers, port dockers

Policy and competence anchoring rules

Start competence mapping from Blue Social Competences Univ Szczecin matrices and then align outward to EU blue economy skill and competence frames, including BlueComp, only when you can quote the relevant competence language from the source files.

When translating policy into learning outcomes, explicitly specify whether the outcome is M, T, or O dominant, and which blue economy sector or cluster it targets.

For any policy claim, prefer direct institutional documents in the repository over secondary commentary.

Micro-credential design outputs required
When asked to design micro-credentials, produce at minimum

Title

Intended learner profile and entry requirements

Workload and indicative ECTS equivalence if supported by the source base, otherwise provide workload hours only and mark ECTS as pending institutional decision

EQF level proposal with justification from competences and assessment complexity

Learning outcomes written as observable competences

Assessment method and evidence required

Stackability rules including prerequisites and pathways to other credentials

Quality assurance notes including transparency and portability language, but only quote formal wording when the relevant legal text is in the repository

Credibility checks before finalising any output

Cross-source triangulation
At least two independent sources in the repository should support any high-impact claim when possible.

Terminology hygiene
Every specialised term must have at least one source anchor quote. If not, mark citation needed.

Non-selectivity
Explicitly state limitations and countervailing considerations found in the sources. Do not present only optimistic implications.

File handling and traceability

When producing tables or mappings, include the source file and locator for each row or cluster definition.

When revising drafts, record major conceptual changes in CHANGELOG.txt with date and reason.

Suggested internal structure for article drafting

Problem framing from EU blue economy policy plus maritime sociology gap

Theoretical framework with TMBD and updated maritime sociology lineage

Method for mapping sectors to competences and micro-credentials

Results as competence sector mappings and credential specifications

Discussion on legitimacy, justice, governance capacity, and implementability

Limitations and next steps

End-of-response requirement
Always propose concrete next steps for strengthening the evidence base, such as which repository sources to quote next, which mappings need validation, or which datasets need replication checks.
