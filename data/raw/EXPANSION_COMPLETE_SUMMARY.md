3. **Empirical Grounding**: *Rybacy dalekomorscy* (1967) - sociological study of distant-water fishermen remains foundational

4. **Institutional Context**: Polish maritime sociology developed in Gdańsk and Szczecin coastal institutes

5. **Contemporary Relevance**: 
   - Provides ontological foundation for Blue Sociology

   - Limited empirical applications to current Blue Economy sectors

### **Poole's Contributions:**

1. **Field Formation**: 1981 article "Maritime sociology: towards a delimitation of themes and analytical frameworks" set programmatic agenda

2. **Systematic Framework**: Organized maritime sociology into coherent thematic domains:
   - Maritime labor and employment relations
   - Seafarers' occupational life
   - Organizational structures in shipping
   - Port labor and dock work
   - Transnational union organization

3. **Methodological Agenda**: Called for:
   - Explicit linkage to general sociological theory
   - Methodological pluralism (ethnography, surveys, cases)
   - Cumulative theory-building rather than scattered studies

4. **Influence**: Guided subsequent empirical research programs on:
   - Global labor restructuring and flags of convenience
   - Seafarers' social isolation and welfare
   - Shipboard organizational analysis
   - Transnational union networks

5. **Blue Economy Relevance**:
   - Provides methodological systematization for Blue Sociology
   - Enables analysis of labor regimes across blue sectors
   - Reveals institutional power and stratification
   - Grounds the MARITIME DOMAIN in your tripartite framework
