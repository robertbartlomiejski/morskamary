Conceptualise the comprehensive and coherent framework, that is novelty extension of Ludwik Janiszewski’s semi-deterministic theory (the interplay of the Marine Factor and Maritime Factor) into a contemporary, inspired and supporting with social dimension to One Ocean Manifesto, One Ocean Pact, High Seas Treaty and Blue Planet Frameworks three-dimensional model suited for the Anthropocene and the Blue Economy. The Tripartite Model of Blue Dynamics model should consist of:

1. Marine Factor ("czynnik morski") - The MARINE Domain (The Biophysical/Ecological Involuntary Agency) -- Process of Marinisation: The capacity of the hydrosphere (ocean, tides, climate) to shape human life, impose limits, and dictate survival conditions -- marine factor in making has three dimensions: de-marinisation (the crisis where biodiversity collapse, through pollution, acidification, carbon-emission ... the ocean's involuntary agency through tides, waves, rainfall, humidity, nutrient metabolic circulation, circularity of water cycle), re-marinisation (the regenerative fix), trans-corporate marinisation (the sea marinise the littoral space by circularity of water, nutritions, tides, humidity, wet-/ dry-, Golfstrom, ...). 



The MARITIME Domain (The Techno-Economic/Institutional/ Infrastructural/ Digital): "ased on Janiszewski’s "Human Factor" (reinterpreted as "Maritime Factor") – Humans terraforming the sea.



Process of Maritimisation: The institutional, economic, and technological colonisation of the sea (ports, fleets, energy grids, laws).

De-maritimisation (The Loss):

Definition: The decline of traditional "People of the Sea." The rusting of old port infrastructure, the gentrification of working waterfronts into luxury housing, and the loss of intergenerational maritime skills (the "knowledge gap").

Impact on Blue Value: Alienation of coastal communities. If locals feel "sea-blindness" and have no economic stake in the ocean, they resist Blue Economy projects (e.g., protesting offshore wind).

Re-maritimisation (The Socio-Economic Revival):

Definition: The Sustainable Blue Economy. Creating new maritime occupations (wind technicians, algae farmers, blue biotech scientists) that provide dignity and livelihood. It involves "locking in" new institutional pathways (Marine Spatial Planning) that prioritise local labour and community benefits."

Goal: Moving from alienation of human from nature (anthropocene) towards transcorporate= giving way to those who have no voice, self-restraint, defending the interests of nature in the name of our shared habitat as a supra-human good.
